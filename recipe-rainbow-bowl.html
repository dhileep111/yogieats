Rainbow Buddha Bowl
with Tahini Dressing

A colorful, complete meal that nourishes your body with plant-based proteins, healthy fats, and vibrant vegetables

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Cook Time: 25 minutes
  • Total Time: 45 minutes
  • Servings: 2 bowls
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Diet: Vegan, Gluten-Free

About This Recipe

This Rainbow Buddha Bowl is a celebration of color, flavor, and nutrition. Each ingredient is thoughtfully chosen to create a balanced, satisfying meal that fuels your body and delights your senses. The combination of protein-rich quinoa, fresh vegetables, and creamy tahini dressing makes this bowl both wholesome and delicious.

Buddha bowls are incredibly versatileâ€”feel free to swap vegetables based on what's in season or what you have on hand. The key is to include a variety of colors, textures, and food groups for optimal nutrition and visual appeal.

Ingredients

For the Bowl:

  • 1 cup quinoa, uncooked (or 3 cups cooked quinoa)
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup shredded purple cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas (or 1 can, drained and rinsed)
  • 1/2 cup edamame, cooked
  • Fresh greens (spinach, kale, or mixed greens)
  • Sesame seeds for garnish
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro or parsley)

For the Tahini Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup or honey
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2-3 tablespoons water (to thin)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1/2 teaspoon cumin for extra flavor

Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Quinoa

Rinse quinoa under cold water. In a medium saucepan, combine quinoa and water (or broth) and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes until liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat, fluff with a fork, and let cool slightly.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables

While quinoa cooks, wash and prepare all vegetables. Shred the cabbage and carrots, slice the cucumber and avocado, halve the cherry tomatoes, and roughly chop fresh herbs.

Step 3: Make the Tahini Dressing

In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, maple syrup, minced garlic, and water. Start with 2 tablespoons of water and add more as needed to reach your desired consistency. The dressing should be creamy and pourable. Season with salt, pepper, and cumin if using.

Step 4: Assemble the Bowls

Divide quinoa between two large bowls as the base. Arrange vegetables in sections around the bowl: purple cabbage, carrots, tomatoes, cucumber, avocado, chickpeas, and edamame. Add a handful of fresh greens.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Drizzle the tahini dressing generously over each bowl. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and fresh herbs. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Nutrition Benefits

This Buddha bowl is a nutritional powerhouse:

  • Quinoa provides complete protein with all nine essential amino acids
  • Chickpeas and edamame add plant-based protein and fiber
  • Avocado offers healthy monounsaturated fats
  • Colorful vegetables deliver vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants
  • Tahini is rich in calcium, magnesium, and healthy fats

Chef's Tips

  • Meal Prep: Cook quinoa and prepare vegetables in advance. Store separately and assemble bowls when ready to eat.
  • Protein Options: Add roasted tofu, tempeh, or your favorite plant-based protein.
  • Seasonal Swaps: Use seasonal vegetables like roasted sweet potato, beets, or Brussels sprouts.
  • Dressing Variations: Try peanut sauce, ginger-miso dressing, or lemon-herb vinaigrette.
  • Storage: Store components separately for up to 3 days. Add avocado fresh before serving.
